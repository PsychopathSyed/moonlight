# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Internal operations team at an event-rental / event-services company. Desktop-first power users who live in this system all day, reading fast and often under time pressure (on-site prep, back-to-back bookings, end-of-month billing). They know the workflow and value speed, muscle memory, and accuracy over hand-holding.

Primary tasks on any given screen: manage inventory & rentals, customers & partners, quotations, invoices & the ledger, expenses, HR, purchase & returns, and gate passes — quote, book, bill, and track stock end-to-end without context-switching across spreadsheets.

## Product Purpose

A single operational ERP for an event-rental/services business. Unifies sales (quotations/invoices), operations (inventory, rentals, gate passes), procurement (purchase/returns), finance (ledger/expenses), and people (HR) so staff can run the whole business from one trusted, auditable system. Success = fewer spreadsheets, fewer double-entry mistakes, faster quote-to-cash, and stock the team can trust.

## Brand Personality

Professional, calm, efficient.

Voice: trustworthy, no hype, gets out of the way. The UI reads like a well-run back office, not a marketing site. Copy is literal and operational; status is always unambiguous. Emotional goal: operator confidence under pressure — the screen never startles, never decorates, never gets in the way of the next transaction.

## Anti-references

- Marketing-style landing chrome applied to an app (oversized hero type, giant CTAs, scroll-driven storytelling).
- Purple/violet AI gradients, gradient text, mesh/aurora backgrounds.
- Glassmorphism, frosted-blur cards, floating-glow shadows.
- Decorative motion, hover-scale parallax, auto-rotating carousels.
- Generic "boost your productivity" SaaS pastel dashboards and toy-ish iconography.
- Flat gray-on-gray palettes with no hierarchy, or rainbow status colors that ignore semantic meaning.

## Design Principles

- **Show, don't tell.** Surface real data — stock levels, balances, statuses, overdue flags — over marketing copy. A number on screen beats a sentence about it.
- **Practice what you preach.** The product sells operational discipline; its own UI must be disciplined, consistent, and auditable. Every record behaves the same way.
- **Expert confidence.** Designed for repeat operators who already know the workflow. Optimize for speed and muscle memory, not onboarding theatre.
- **Calm under pressure.** Clear hierarchy and restraint over decoration, so operators can read fast and act accurately during busy runs.
- **One source of truth.** Each record — a rental, an invoice, a stock item — has one authoritative, traceable view. Never two numbers that should match but don't.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Full keyboard navigation with visible focus, semantic tables and forms, AA contrast for all text and status indicators, and `prefers-reduced-motion` support. Data-dense screens (tables, ledgers) are first-class for screen readers, not afterthoughts.
